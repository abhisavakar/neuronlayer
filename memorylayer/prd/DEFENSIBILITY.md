## Real-World Examples

### Products That Were "Easy to Build" But Won

| Product | Technical Difficulty | Why They Won |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|
| Dropbox | Easy (file sync existed) | Marketing, UX, brand, trust |
| Notion | Medium (wiki + database) | Community, templates, word-of-mouth |
| Linear | Medium (issue tracker) | Design, speed, developer love |
| Superhuman | Easy (email client) | Waitlist hype, premium positioning |
| Basecamp | Easy (project management) | Content marketing, strong opinions |
| Figma | Medium (design tool) | Collaboration, browser-based |

### What They Had in Common

1. **First or best marketing** - Not first or best code
2. **Strong brand identity** - Memorable, distinct
3. **Community/word-of-mouth** - Users who love them
4. **Relentless iteration** - Kept improving
5. **Strong opinions** - Stood for something

### What They Did NOT Have

- Proprietary algorithms
- Uncopiable technology
- Patents protecting them
- Exclusive access to resources
